Land use

Learn more about Amsterdam's trend toward a more livable and sustainable urban environment and how land use is related to people's preferred mode of transportation.

The city of Amsterdam has made effort to prioritize bike infrastructure in order to make cycling the primary mode of transportation within the city. This concentrated effort can be seen on the ways land is used around Amsterdam. This effort has been driven by the need for a more sustainable but also more livable urban environment.

There are variety of measures implemented to make the city easy, safe and convenient for cyclists and pedestrians. It has been even argued that Amsterdam’s bike infrastructure is so well-designed and safe, that nobody wears helmets.

Overall, the changes in land use in Amsterdam reflect a larger trend towards sustainable and livable urban environments. By prioritizing cycling, Amsterdam has created a more attractive, healthier, and more vibrant city.

Information

Mixed land use

Amsterdam has compact urban form meaning that everything is relatively a short distance away, which makes biking quick and efficient. Many neighbourhoods in Amsterdam are mixed-use with residential, commercial and institutional buildings close to each other. This makes biking or walking to a shop or a school from your home easiest mode of transport.

Bike lanes

Extensive network of bike lanes are separated from the motor vehicle traffic with physical barriers. Bike lanes are also clearly marked with different colored roads and signs. There are also several streets were cars are allowed but cyclists have priority.

Car-free zones

In the city center many areas are also completely car-free, such as the Dam Square or the Red Light District. Walking and biking is therefore prioritized in this areas, making them calmer and safer. In other areas traffic is calmed with reduced speed limits, narrow roads and traffic circles.
