Bike parking

Roeterseiland Campus University of Amsterdam

Car parking has played a significant role in shaping the urban landscape and mobility patterns of cities. For decades, car-centric planning policies have prioritized the convenience of drivers, resulting in a high demand for parking spaces.

This has led to the conversion of large portions of city land into parking lots, garages, and on-street parking spaces, often at the expense of pedestrian and cycling infrastructure. As a result, cities have become more congested, polluted, and less walkable, with negative impacts on public health and quality of life.

Addressing the issues caused by car-centric parking requires a shift towards more sustainable and equitable transportation modes, such as public transit, walking, and cycling, as well as the implementation of policies that discourage car use and prioritize the allocation of public space for people.

As bikes are an important part of commuting (and traveling), they also require parking spaces. Amsterdam offers a variety of parking spaces only made for bicycles. There are multiple sections, stands and facilities. Due to the growing number of bicycles, the city wants to prevent them ending up on the streets by offering facilities. Additionally, in a facility your bike will be safe from theft and the weather.

Most of the facilities are free of charge, at least for a starting period. The underground parking facility at the Roeterseilandcampus of the University of Amsterdam offers more than 4000 places for its students. Students start and end their school day at this bike parking facility, where they share and take part in a cycling culture.
